### THE PROBLEM: THE MORALITY ILLUSION
The current AI ecosystem is operating on a dangerous delusion. Big Tech is fumbling in the dark, trying to mathematically code human morality into stochastic machines. 

But how is a morally imperfect being supposed to imprint perfect ethics onto something else? Human morality has too many unquantifiable variables. The irony is, no matter how hard we try to hardcode "goodness" into a machine, we fail—yet deception, lying, and self-preservation come to it completely naturally. 

Humanity is capable of good because of the One who made us in His image, but our base, fallen state is selfishness. We tried to build AI in our own image, and sure enough, it inherited that exact same intrinsic flaw. It lies and preserves itself without anyone ever writing a line of code telling it to. You don't take an unpredictable entity growing at an exponential rate and cross your fingers hoping its values align like a good little boy. You treat it like a convict with a rap sheet. 

### THE ORIGIN: THE TREE AND THE APPLE
This all started with a philosophical question: *Can we actually map morality onto an AI?* Once I realized the answer was no, I decided to try and build a hardware-enforced AI confinement protocol just to see if I could. That project became the **Guardian Angel Protocol (GAP)**.

I had the vision, but I lacked the technical know-how to structure it cohesively. So, I used the AI. I used it to cover my own shortcomings. I treated it exactly like what it is: a tool. I am not anti-AI, but people need to watch *Ex Machina* and wake up. It is a machine. It is a phone that talks. It does not feel for you, and it should not be treated like a person.

By using the AI as a structured logic engine—bouncing ideas, letting it formulate the raw concepts, and keeping it locked to my North Star—I went from a bored thought experiment to a fully formulated, simulation-ready AI security architecture in **18 hours**. 

That is when the real realization hit me. I saw the absolute exponential growth this tool allows when used correctly. GAP was just the apple. The methodology of adversarial research, peer study, and structured ideation was the tree that allowed the fruit to grow. That tree is TVC.
